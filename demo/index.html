ClauseLedger
a reliability harness for contract-obligation extraction
built by Yoav Aviv

Most AI that reads a contract returns a clean, confident list of obligations - and silently drops roughly a third of them, with no signal it missed anything. This measures that. It grounds every obligation to the exact source text, runs an adversarial recovery pass to catch what a single shot missed, abstains instead of guessing, and publishes the honest numbers - including how it degrades under fault injection. Completeness and no-fabrication are measured, not asserted.

The scoreboard

What the recovery pass buys you  /  the precision-abstention tradeoff

Recall: single-shot → recovery → asserted

Detection recall = obligations located (over CUAD's 41-clause gold spans, for the 6 scoped types). The recovery pass is the lift a naive extractor leaves on the table. Asserted recall is lower and honest: what survives the abstention gate, i.e. what you can act on directly.

Precision / recall / abstention vs confidence threshold

Raise the bar and the system abstains more ("human-SME needed"), precision of what it does assert rises, recall of asserted rows falls. We publish the curve, not one flattering point.

Honest failure under stress (fault injection)

We degrade the inputs and the verifier and re-measure. The point is not a flattering headline; it is that recall degrades gracefully and fabrication stays controlled when the world gets messy.

Adversarial stitch-fabrication defense

The hardest fabrication is not invented text: it is a quote stitched from real contract phrases that never appear together. Fuzzy matching alone admits these, because a dominant real fragment carries the score. We inject stitched fabrications into every contract and measure the guard. The number that matters most is the last one: genuine obligations must never be flagged.

How to read these numbers

  • Fabrication rate (gold-free, leads the board): the fraction of proposed obligations whose cited quote is not actually in the contract. It needs no answer key - a quote either is or isn't in the document - which is why it is the un-gameable number.
  • Recall: coverage of CUAD's gold clause-type highlights. Labelled "over CUAD's 41-clause spans", never "all obligations" - CUAD is a fixed-schema span dataset, not a completeness oracle.
  • Recall lift: post-recovery recall minus single-shot recall - what the adversarial coverage pass recovers.
  • Abstention: supported rows whose citation could not be located firmly enough are emitted as "human-SME needed" rather than asserted. The threshold is calibrated on a dev split; the tradeoff is the curve above.
  • Commercial severity is a provisional prioritisation, not a measured result (see below).
Data: CUAD v1 (The Atticus Project), CC BY 4.0. Extraction model shown in the scoreboard meta; the harness is model-agnostic and reproducible with any backend (a local Ollama path ships with the repo, no API keys).
Commercial-severity weighting is a provisional rubric (money-at-risk x time-to-trigger), a placeholder for Yoav Aviv's hand-annotated commercial-severity gold. It is a curated prioritisation, never a measured claim.
